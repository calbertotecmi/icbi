What is the Positive Museums project?

It is an initiative that emerged in 2017 as part of the collaboration between the International Santa Luc√≠a Festival and the Institute for Wellbeing and Happiness, that seeks to join art and people through the experience of positive emotions. Through Positive Museums, Universidad Tecmilenio, recognized as the first positive university in the world, contributes to the positive transformation of community life through teaching skills that promote individual and collective well-being.



Positive Museum

A Positive Museum transforms the experience of the museum so visitors identify and practice positive emotions. It is carried out through workshops, guided tours and activities that enable them to create engagement with themselves and with others, creating an atmosphere of well-being at the same time.

All experiences in a Positive Museum have a scientific foundation based on the science of Positive Psychology (Seligman, 2016) and the approach of Art as Therapy (De Botton & Armstrong, 2017).

Wax Museum
Strengths in Wax
Tour in which the participants inquire and discover the strengths of the most emblematic characters in Universal History who are remembered by their contributions to the world, naming at least one strength of each of the selected characters as part of the activity; at the end of the tour, the participants carry out a workshop where, through a process of self-analysis, they identify their own strengths and model them in clay sharing with the other participants.

Candy Museum
Sweet memories
Guided tour where the participants travel along time and space, re-living memories from childhood in the old and traditional Monterrey, bringing back moments of happiness from the past; there is also a tasting carried out, where they are invited to reflect on the positive emotions, the reason why candy is related to something good; they also share positive anecdotes they remembered during the tour and portray them in a message that is accompanied by exchanging candy with the other participants.
Carried out in: 2019

Emotions over time
Guided tour where participants are asked to observe the different art works of a permanent collection and select one of their liking. Once the selection is made, they will create a drawing will and a reflection thinking about the emotion that the person who used the object in the past could have experienced, how does the history of the object make them feel in the present and what recommendations could be made to a participant in the future so there is a more special attention given to the object, to appreciate and value it.
In this way, time, space and people are connected through positive emotions.
Carried out in: 2017

My positive memories at "El Obispado"
Tour through the Photographic Memory of Mexico exhibit, where participant learn about events, characters, constructions and daily life in the XX Century in Mexico. Participants select one photograph out of the ones presented in the exhibition and on a little face representing one of the 10 positive emotions, they write a brief message regarding the emotion they experienced looking at the selected photograph; finally, they place the little face in the emotions mailbox.
Carried out in: 2018
Papalote Children Museum
The Pathway to Happiness
Customization of the classical board game snakes and ladders designed to build happy moments and strengthen relationships with friends, families or work peers, using positive emotions as enablers for happiness.
In the game, the players will use positive emotions to achieve the challenges; each achieved challenge, the players will receive positive emotions cards, which they will be able to collect during the game so, later on, they can exchange to go up a ladder or avoid falling through a snake, learning the importance of emotional balance to achieve well-being.
Carried out in: 2017
Papalote Children Museum
Messages
Activity where the participants write messages that express their wishes, engagements, emotions and feelings in favor of environmental preservation on pieces of paper representing the 10 positive emotions and with the support of a Cousin, they place their messages in the Wishing tree.
Carried out in: 2018, 2019
Alfa Planetarium and Dialogue in the Dark
Bring your emotions to the light
Together with the exhibition "Dialogue in the dark" there is a sensorial tour created called "Bring your emotions to the light", through which a unique learning experience is lived in total darkness, seeking that the participants make contact with their emotions and explore ways to transform negative emotions such as fear or frustration into hope and gratitude; at the end of the tour, the participants shares the experience as a luminous message over a wall so the other participants may feel inspired.
Carried out in: 2018
Alfa Planetarium
Exploring senses and emotions
Sensorial tour through the aviary and gardens of ALFA Planetarium. Through the senses, the participants discover emotions that emerge while being in contact with nature, and then depict on a stone or piece of paper the positive emotion they felt during the tour.
Carried out in: 2019
